<p><ul><li>Intrusive</li><li>Non-invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pediatric electronic ventilator market is divided into two main types: intrusive and non-invasive. Intrusive ventilators involve the use of endotracheal tubes or tracheostomy for direct airway access, providing precise control over ventilation in critically ill infants and children. In contrast, non-invasive ventilators use masks or nasal interfaces, offering a gentler approach that reduces the risk of infection and discomfort. Both types are crucial in managing respiratory disorders in pediatric patients, catering to diverse clinical needs and preferences.</p></p>
