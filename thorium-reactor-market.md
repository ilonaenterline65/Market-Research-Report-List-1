<p><p>The Thorium Reactor market encompasses various reactor types, each utilizing thorium fuel differently. Heavy Water Reactors (PHWRs) leverage heavy water for neutron moderation, while High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactors (HTRs) use helium as a coolant. Boiling Water Reactors (BWRs) generate steam directly in the reactor core, and Pressurized Water Reactors (PWRs) keep water under pressure to prevent boiling. Fast Neutron Reactors (FNRs) utilize fast neutrons, Molten Salt Reactors (MSRs) use molten salts as coolant, and Accelerator Driven Reactors (ADS) integrate particle accelerators to sustain reactions efficiently.</p></p>
