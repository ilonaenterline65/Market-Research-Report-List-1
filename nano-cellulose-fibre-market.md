<p><p>The nano-cellulose fibre market comprises three main types: Cellulose Nano Fibrils (CNF), which are long, flexible fibers offering reinforcement in composites; Cellulose Nano Crystals (CNC), characterized by their rigid structure and high strength, ideal for enhancing material properties; and Bacterial Nanocellulose (BNC), produced by bacteria, known for its purity and unique gel-like properties, suitable for medical and cosmetic applications. Together, these variants cater to diverse industries, including packaging, construction, and healthcare, highlighting their versatility and sustainable nature.</p></p>
