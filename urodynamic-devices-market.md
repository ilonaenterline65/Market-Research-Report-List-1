<p><p>The urodynamic devices market comprises various types essential for assessing urinary tract function. Ambulatory urodynamic devices allow patients to undergo testing in a non-hospital setting. Cystometers measure bladder pressure and volume during filling. Video urodynamic devices combine video imaging with pressure measurements for detailed analysis. Electromyography evaluates the muscular responses of the pelvic floor. Urodynamic catheters facilitate fluid flow during tests, while urodynamic pumps regulate fluid rates. Transducer sets measure pressure changes, enhancing diagnostic accuracy in urodynamics.</p></p>
