<p><p>The self-cleaning architectural membrane market comprises several key types, each with unique properties. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) offers excellent UV resistance and low surface energy, making it highly effective for self-cleaning. Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF) is known for its durability and chemical resistance. Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) is cost-effective with good flexibility. Ethylene-Tetra-Fluoro-Ethylene (ETFE) is lightweight, highly transparent, and has a long lifespan. Additionally, "Others" encompasses emerging materials that may combine attributes of existing types or introduce novel self-cleaning capabilities.</p></p>
