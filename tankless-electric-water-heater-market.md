<p><p>The tankless electric water heater market is primarily divided into two types: whole-house and point-of-use. Whole-house tankless water heaters are designed to supply hot water for an entire home, providing a continuous flow without the need for a storage tank, thus saving space and energy. Point-of-use tankless heaters, on the other hand, are installed near specific fixtures, delivering hot water directly at the source for quick access and reduced heat loss during transit, making them ideal for specific applications.</p></p>
