<p><p>The double-lumen central venous catheter market consists of various types, including jugular vein catheters, subclavian vein catheters, femoral vein catheters, and others. Jugular vein catheters are inserted into the internal jugular vein, providing excellent access for treatments. Subclavian vein catheters are placed under the clavicle, offering stability and minimal infection risk. Femoral vein catheters are inserted in the thigh, often used in emergencies. Other types may include peripheral insertion or specialized catheters for unique clinical needs.</p></p>
