<p><p>Surrogacy services primarily include two market types: gestational surrogacy and traditional surrogacy. In gestational surrogacy, the surrogate carries an embryo created through in vitro fertilization (IVF), allowing the intended parents to retain genetic links; this method is the most common in the market. In traditional surrogacy, the surrogate is artificially inseminated with the intended father's sperm, making her the biological mother of the child. Both types cater to varying needs of intended parents, influencing legal frameworks and ethical considerations.</p></p>
